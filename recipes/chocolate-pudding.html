Chocolate Pudding

Description

Chocolate puddings are a class of desserts in the pudding family with chocolate flavors. There are two main types: a boiled then chilled dessert, texturally a custard set with starch, commonly eaten in the U.S., Canada, Germany, Sweden, Poland, and East and South East Asia; and a steamed/baked version, texturally similar to cake, popular in the UK, Ireland, Australia, Germany and New Zealand.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup baking cocoa
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • OPTIONAL: Sweetened whipped cream

Steps

  1. In a heavy saucepan, combine sugar, cocoa, cornstarch and salt.
  2. Gradually add milk. Bring to a boil over medium heat; boil and stir for 2 minutes.
  3. Remove from the heat; stir in butter and vanilla. Spoon into individual serving dishes.
  4. Chill until serving. If desired, garnish with whipped cream.
